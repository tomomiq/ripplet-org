At first glance, one could say this structure is almost confusing in its obvious-ness. It’s a host conducting an interview, and incorporating questions from the audience. However this interpretation would be a disservice to this structure. I believe its power lies in bringing it into a situation where the classic choice would be for the Celebrity to be handed a mic to talk. 

## Facilitation notes

The “questions from the audience” part includes several, subtle twists. Questions are co-developed by multiple members. The host is responsible for curating and contextualizing how it's asked to the Celebrity, _after_ the first half of the interview has been conducted. The exchange tends to be richer, as questions of a narrower range or interest, as well as topics that have already been covered, are weeded out. There is also no space for the "this is more of a comment" type of audience input. Great! 

## Workshop design notes

I like the idea of using the label of Celebrity Interview to signal that something special/different is going to happen, for everyone involved.
