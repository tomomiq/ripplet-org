A few minutes of tongue twisters and the equivalent of scales on the piano for your voice will

* Raise your energy level
* Loosen up your throat and jaw
* Correct your posture out of a slouch

People talk about breathwork to re-claim focus but I find vocalizing to be more effective. I like to putter around making tea, stretching my upper body and doing a bit of vocalizing. The idea is to get the mumbling and stumbling around for words out of my system before speaking with another human being.

**When it's especially important:**

* Before your first call in the morning
* After a few hours of not talking (ex. writing)
* Before a presentation

**Try a bunch, find a few favorites.**

Think back to your past drama school or singing lessons, or hop onto YouTube for inspiration. If you speak multiple languages, have fun with that, too.

**Start your next call, all warmed up.**
