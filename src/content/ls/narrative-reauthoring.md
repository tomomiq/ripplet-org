I was wholly seeped into this structure in our [Studio](<https://academy.betterworktogether.co/p/liberating-structures-studio>) and walked out with a vivid story that had elements from different dreams embedded in it. Dreams I hadn’t dared to face as things I yearned for. 

## In practice

I'm curious to try journaling with Lynda Barry's [invitation](<https://thenearsightedmonkey.tumblr.com/post/17248396240/dear-my-great-what-it-is-class-re-stories>) that inspired this structure, using the video she’s published for it to be a self-serve activity. 

The application in my professional context is harder to imagine. Narrative reauthoring would be a beautiful experience to host at a retreat but that kind of gathering is maybe one out of ten workshops that I run.

But the concept is so seductive. I could see it being developed into an incredibly powerful pattern — something to think about after a few more experiences! 

## Facilitation notes

A note to my future self that may facilitate this -

Having your story read out loud by someone is a touching experience. After being in a meditative state with the facilitator's tone as the sole aural signal, you hear a new voice read the words that you just pulled together from your imagination. Words that have never been spoken before.

My partner did that, unexpectedly, and I'm grateful for it. I did the same, and found that forming her text in my mouth and listening to the sounds myself brought a different appreciation than I might have if I had just read them on the screen. 

I would make this part of the instructions.
