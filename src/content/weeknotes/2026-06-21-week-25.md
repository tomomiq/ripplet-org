It's been a rough week, physically, as I battle jetlag, a heatwave and tree pollen allergy. The heatwave will continue next week, so I've changed the duvet and dug out the fan, ice cube makers, ice pillow etc. The pollen is a huge energy suck - it makes me feel dazed and weak - so I'll be more conscientious next week with meds, the air purifier and changing from outside clothes. Time to get pro-active. 

Nevertheless, it's been super nice to be home and back in Paris. My highlight was the [Hilma af Klint show](https://www.grandpalais.fr/en/program/hilma-af-klint-paintings-temple-1906-1915
) at the Grand Palais, titled "Paintings for the Temple (1906-1915)". 

I've been captivated by her life story and the "discovery" of her work in recent decades, and even read the excellent [Julia Voss biography](https://www.amazon.fr/Hilma-Klint-Biography-Julia-Voss/dp/022668976X) last year with faith that I would have a chance to see her work live one day! And as is the beauty of living in Paris, I encountered exhibition posters at the metro station on Monday and promptly booked a ticket for the next day. 

On the way home, I picked up another book, also by Voss, which draws the parallels between af Klint and Kandinsky.
